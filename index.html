SMR Rectifier Repair & Power Electronics Maintenance

Comprehensive repair and maintenance of Switch Mode Rectifier (SMR) systems, including fault diagnosis, component replacement, and performance optimization for telecommunications and industrial power systems.

Common SMR Faults & Repair Procedures:
  • Input Stage Issues: Bridge rectifier failures, inrush current limiter faults, EMI filter problems
  • Power Factor Correction (PFC): PFC MOSFET/Diode failures, PFC controller IC issues, boost inductor overheating
  • DC-DC Conversion: Primary switching MOSFET failures, transformer issues, output rectifier problems
  • Control & Monitoring: Microcontroller faults, feedback circuit failures, communication interface issues
  • Cooling System: Fan failures, heatsink thermal issues, thermal protection circuit faults
SMR Repair & Testing Procedure:
1 Initial Diagnosis & Safety Check

Visual inspection for burnt components, bulging capacitors, and physical damage. Verify input/output isolation and check for short circuits.

2 Input Stage Testing

Test bridge rectifier, inrush current limiter, and EMI filter components. Verify AC input voltage handling capability and leakage current.

3 PFC Circuit Analysis

Check PFC MOSFETs, diodes, and controller IC. Measure power factor and verify PFC output voltage stability under varying loads.

4 DC-DC Converter Repair

Test primary switching components, gate drive circuits, and output rectifiers. Verify transformer integrity and check for winding shorts.

5 Control Circuit Troubleshooting

Diagnose microcontroller, voltage reference, and feedback circuits. Verify protection circuits (OVP, OCP, OTP) functionality.

6 Performance Verification

Test efficiency, load regulation, line regulation, and transient response. Verify thermal performance and acoustic noise levels.

7 Final Testing & Burn-in

Conduct extended load testing, thermal cycling, and environmental stress testing to ensure long-term reliability.

PV System Commissioning

Commissioning of photovoltaic systems including installation verification, performance testing, and system optimization for maximum efficiency.

Eltek Solar Pack System Commissioning Procedure:
1 Pre-Commissioning Inspection

Verify all mechanical and electrical installations are complete and correct. Check for proper grounding, cable routing, and component mounting.

2 DC System Verification

Measure open-circuit voltage and short-circuit current of PV strings. Verify polarity and check for earth faults. Ensure string fuses are correctly rated.

3 AC System Verification

Check AC voltage and frequency at the grid connection point. Verify proper operation of isolation devices and protection equipment.

4 Inverter Initialization

Power up the Eltek inverter following manufacturer sequence. Configure system parameters according to site requirements.

5 System Performance Testing

Monitor system operation under various load conditions. Verify maximum power point tracking (MPPT) efficiency and grid synchronization.

6 Safety System Verification

Test all safety features including anti-islanding protection, over/under voltage protection, and emergency shutdown procedures.

7 Data Monitoring Setup

Configure remote monitoring system. Verify data logging accuracy and communication with central monitoring platform.

8 Final Documentation

Complete commissioning report with all test results, system parameters, and as-built documentation for client handover.
